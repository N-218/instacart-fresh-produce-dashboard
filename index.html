This dashboard looks at how people on Instacart buy fresh produce and what makes them order the same items again. It shows which products are bought the most, when customers usually shop, and how their buying habits change over time. A simple model is also included to estimate the chance of an item being reordered. All of this helps give Instacart a clearer picture of customer behaviour in the fresh produce category.

  • How reorders change over time
  • What affects repeat buying
  • Reorder predictions

03 • Story & Approach
Why fresh matters – and how the analysis was done

Why fresh produce matters

Fresh produce affects how customers feel about Instacart because they expect good quality. Understanding how people buy these items can help improve recommendations and stock planning.

Data & method

The project uses the public Instacart dataset from Kaggle. Only fresh produce items were selected. The data was cleaned and combined in Python. After exploring the main patterns, a simple Random Forest model was created to estimate how likely items are to be reordered.

Analytical lenses used

The analysis moves from descriptive to diagnostic, then into predictive and finally prescriptive insights.

D Descriptive: volumes, timing, distributions Dg Diagnostic: loyalty vs velocity, cohorts P Predictive: Random Forest reorder model Pr Prescriptive: recommendations & next steps
04 • Model Overview
Random Forest reorder model – what the machine learnt

Confusion Matrix

Shows how often the model correctly and incorrectly predicts reorders versus non-reorders, giving a transparent view of misclassification.

Feature Importance

Highlights which customer, product, and user–product features the model relies on most when predicting reorders.

Model summary

The model predicts if a customer may reorder a fresh item. It takes into consideration how popular the item is, how the customer normally shops, and whether they bought the item before..

Performance

The ROC–AUC score of 0.794 shows how well the model ranks items by their chance of being reordered. The confusion matrix and feature-importance chart help explain what the model pays most attention to.

05 • Recommendations
What Instacart should actually do
  • Show key fresh items clearly in the “Buy Again” area.
  • Use typical reorder timing to send useful reminders.
  • Plan stock and staffing around busy fresh hours.
  • Review items that sell a lot but get few reorders.
  • Suggest reliable fresh items to new customers.
06 • Limitations
What this project doesn’t cover
  • The dataset is older and may not match current behaviour.
  • Only fresh produce items were included.
  • The model is basic and could be improved with more information.
07 • Technical Notes
Tools, stack & governance

Python was used for the data work, mainly pandas for cleaning and scikit-learn for the model. Plotly was used for the charts. The dashboard was built with HTML, CSS and JavaScript so everything could be kept in one file. The Instacart dataset used here is anonymised and publicly available on Kaggle.

08 • AI Reflection
Working with AI in the project

AI tools were used only to help with small problems during the project, mostly when the code wasn’t working or when different options needed to be compared. They made it easier to understand errors and gave ideas for how to approach certain steps.

The actual decisions in the project such as what features to include, how to read the charts and what the results meant for fresh produce shopping were based on my own understanding after looking at the data. Anything suggested by AI was rewritten so it matched my style and made sense for this work. The main insights and recommendations came from exploring the data directly, not from AI. It was simply a helpful tool for fixing issues and checking ideas.
